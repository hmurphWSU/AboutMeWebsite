Academic History

Elementary School

As you could probably expect, I really don't have anything left of the times that I was in elementary school and junior high, but I will still cover where I went to school. As a side note, I know it's not really something that is common, but I did actually go to pre-school at a family friend's pre-school. Continuing on, from kindergarten all the way to 5th grade I went to Municipal Elementary in Roy, Utah. In the middle of 5th grade, I transferred to Quest Academy where I carried out there from grades 5 to 9.

Junior High

At Quest Academy, we did a lot of work online so I would have some work that I could show for my junior high years, however, the accounts that we used that has all of the work we did was on their school domain so I can no longer access it. However, as with most junior highs, you do have the option to take some higher classes that could fulfill high school credits, which I took the opportunity to do. While at Quest Academy, I took Financial Literacy, Health 1 and 2, and Geography for Life which satisfied all of my health credits, my financial literacy credit, and part of my geography credit for the credits that I needed with me going to NUAMES for high school.

High School

As mentioned in the junior high section, I went to NUAMES for years in high school. NUAMES has been around for almost 20 years, being started in Layton, Utah in 2004. However, in the fall of 2018, NUAMES expanded their operations onto the Weber State University campus. This is when I started attending NUAMES. With the Ogden campus only being open since 2018, my graduating class was the first class to attend the Ogden campus throughout all high school years (grades 10 to 12). Since NUAMES is an early college high school, you can take college classes while being in high school, allowing you to graduate with a degree when you graduate high school. I took this option and graduated from NUAMES with an associate's degree in science. I would have something to show classes I have taken, however, as with my junior high, I can no longer access my account that would tell me all of the classes I have taken.
